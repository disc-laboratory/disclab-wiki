# Conference vs Journal Strategy

## General principle

The choice between conference and journal publication should be driven by **scientific fit, maturity of the work, and communication goals**, not only by prestige.

## Use conferences when

- the work is timely and benefits from rapid dissemination;
- early feedback from the community is strategically valuable;
- the venue is relevant to the target research community;
- the work represents a well-defined but possibly intermediate contribution;
- visibility and discussion are priorities.

## Use journals when

- the work is mature, validated, and complete;
- the contribution requires deeper methodological or theoretical development;
- extensive experiments and analysis are needed;
- archival impact and long-term reference value are important.

## Combined strategy

In many cases, a combined approach is appropriate:

- conference → early version, feedback, visibility;
- journal → extended version with deeper validation and contributions.

## Lab recommendation

- prioritize **scientific alignment with the venue** over impact factor alone;
- ensure that the level of rigor matches the target venue;
- avoid fragmenting contributions into multiple weak papers;
- aim for coherent, meaningful contributions rather than maximizing publication count.

## Key consideration

The objective is not only to publish, but to produce **clear, credible, and impactful research contributions**.
