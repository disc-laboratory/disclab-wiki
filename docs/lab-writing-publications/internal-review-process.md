# Internal Review Process

## Objective

The internal review process ensures that all manuscripts meet the lab’s standards in terms of:

- clarity and structure;
- scientific rigor;
- consistency between methods, results, and claims;
- readiness for submission.

## Suggested workflow

1. **Lead author prepares a complete draft**
   - includes full structure, figures, and main message;
   - results and conclusions must be consistent.

2. **Co-authors review**
   - focus on technical correctness and positioning;
   - provide structured and constructive feedback.

3. **PI first review**
   - evaluates scientific contribution, clarity, and overall quality;
   - identifies major issues or required changes.

4. **Revision by lead author**
   - consolidate feedback;
   - ensure consistency across sections.

5. **PI final review**
   - confirms readiness for submission;
   - validates positioning and contribution.

6. **Submission preparation**
   - prepare cover letter and required documents;
   - ensure formatting compliance with venue guidelines.

7. **Final check**
   - verify figures, references, and formatting;
   - confirm all authors approve submission.

8. **Submission**
   - lead author submits with PI approval and credentials.

## Minimum rule

Do not circulate incomplete drafts for broad review if:

- the structure is unclear;
- figures are missing or not interpretable;
- the main message is not defined.

## Expected behavior

- reviews should be timely and constructive;
- feedback should be specific and actionable;
- the lead author is responsible for consolidating and implementing revisions.

The goal is to improve the paper efficiently while maintaining high quality standards.
