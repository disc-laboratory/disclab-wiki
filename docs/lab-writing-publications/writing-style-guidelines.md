# Writing Style Guidelines

## General principle

Scientific writing should prioritize **clarity, precision, and structure** over complexity.

A well-written paper reflects clear thinking and a strong understanding of the problem.

## Key expectations

- write clearly and directly;
- avoid unnecessary complexity;
- ensure every sentence contributes to the message;
- maintain consistency in terminology.

## Structure of a strong paper

A typical paper should clearly communicate:

1. **Context and motivation**
2. **Problem or gap**
3. **Proposed approach**
4. **Validation and results**
5. **Key contributions and implications**

## Writing recommendations

### Clarity

- use simple and precise language;
- avoid overly long sentences;
- define terms when first introduced.

### Consistency

- use consistent terminology throughout the paper;
- avoid switching between synonyms for the same concept;
- ensure figures, text, and tables use the same naming conventions.

### Conciseness

- avoid redundant explanations;
- remove unnecessary words or repetitions;
- focus on what is essential.

### Technical precision

- ensure claims are supported by results;
- avoid vague statements (e.g., “significant improvement” without quantification);
- report relevant metrics and conditions.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- unclear problem statement;
- missing connection between results and conclusions;
- inconsistent terminology;
- overly descriptive but not analytical writing;
- figures that are not explained in the text.

## Final note

Good writing is iterative. Expect to revise multiple times to achieve clarity and coherence.
