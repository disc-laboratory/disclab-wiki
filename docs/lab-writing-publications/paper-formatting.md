# Paper Formatting

## General recommendations

- LaTeX templates are preferred;
- use conference/journal templates when provided;
- otherwise, use the lab paper template as a baseline.

## Figures and tables

- figures must be high quality and clearly readable;
- prefer vectorized figures tools (e.g., inkscape) and formats (e.g., `.pdf`) when possible;
- ensure consistency in style, fonts, and colors;
- use clear naming conventions:
  - figures: `fig_short_description.pdf`
  - tables: `tab_short_description`
- appropriate and ethical use of GenAI for assistance on framework figures is encoraged (e.g., feedback of composition; generation of individual components)

## References

- use consistent citation styles;
- ensure all references are complete and correctly formatted;
- guarantee to have display doi and web link;
- use descriptive naming for `.bib` entries (e.g., `YearAuthor_keyword`).

## Response to reviewers

- address comments **one by one**;
- copy each reviewer comment and respond explicitly;
- use deferent format for reviewers comments and your replies (e.g., italic, blue);
- clearly indicate changes made in the manuscript;
- maintain a professional, respectful and constructive tone;
- it is ecoraged to accept and incorporates as much as possible the reviewers' comments;
- only rebute a comment if it is completely out of context.

## Structure and clarity

- ensure logical flow between sections;
- avoir being overwordy --- simple and straight forward is prefered;
- avoid redundancy;
- make figures and captions self-contained;
- ensure terminology is consistent throughout the paper;
- ensure to define all the acronyms.

## Guidance

Templates should reduce formatting friction, not replace scientific thinking.

Good formatting supports clarity, but the primary focus should always remain on the quality and coherence of the scientific contribution.
