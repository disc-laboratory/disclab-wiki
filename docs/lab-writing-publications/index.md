# Writing and Publications

This section defines the standards, practices, and expectations related to scientific writing and publication within the DISC Laboratory.

The goal is to ensure that research outputs from the group are:

- scientifically rigorous;
- clearly communicated;
- well-structured and reproducible;
- aligned with high academic standards.

## Scope

This section covers:

- manuscript preparation and structuring;
- authorship and contribution principles;
- internal review workflows;
- publication strategies (conference vs journal);
- formatting and presentation of scientific content.

## General philosophy

Writing is considered an integral part of research, not a final step. A well-written paper reflects clear thinking, strong methodology, and careful interpretation of results.

Members are expected to:

- treat writing as part of the research process;
- aim for clarity, precision, and technical correctness;
- maintain consistency between experiments, results, and claims;
- follow structured workflows for drafting and reviewing manuscripts.

## Structure of this section

The documents in this section provide guidance on:

- how to organize and prepare manuscripts;
- how contributions translate into authorship;
- how internal review and submission should be handled;
- how to choose appropriate publication venues;
- how to format papers and supporting materials.

These guidelines are intended to support high-quality output while maintaining consistency across the lab.
