# Reviewing Papers Guidelines

## Purpose

Reviewing papers is an essential part of academic development. It helps improve critical thinking and understanding of research quality.

## General principle

A good review is:

- objective;
- constructive;
- focused on scientific content.

## What to evaluate

### Contribution

- is the problem clearly defined?
- is the contribution meaningful and original?

### Methodology

- is the approach sound?
- are assumptions reasonable?

### Experiments

- are experiments well designed?
- are results convincing and properly validated?

### Clarity

- is the paper well structured?
- is the writing clear and precise?

## Providing feedback

- be specific and actionable;
- distinguish major vs minor comments;
- suggest improvements when possible.

## Tone

- remain professional and respectful;
- avoid dismissive or vague criticism;
- focus on improving the work.

## Internal reviews in the lab

When reviewing papers within the lab:

- prioritize technical correctness;
- ensure consistency between sections;
- check alignment between results and claims.

## Common mistakes

- focusing only on minor issues;
- not identifying core weaknesses;
- providing vague feedback;
- not reading the paper carefully.

## Final note

Reviewing is part of being a researcher. Treat it as a serious and constructive activity.
