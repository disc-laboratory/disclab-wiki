# Research Storytelling

## General principle

A strong paper tells a clear and coherent story about a problem and its solution.

## Key elements

A good research narrative includes:

1. motivation
2. problem definition
3. gap in existing work
4. proposed solution
5. validation
6. implications

## Positioning the contribution

- clearly state what is new;
- explain why it matters;
- relate it to existing work.

## Flow

- each section should logically follow the previous one;
- avoid disconnected ideas;
- maintain a clear thread throughout the paper.

## Common issues

- unclear contribution;
- weak connection between sections;
- excessive technical detail without context;
- lack of focus.

## Final note

A paper is not just a collection of results. It is a structured argument.
