# Expectations — Overview

This section outlines the main expectations for members of the DISC Laboratory. These documents are intended to provide clarity on the professional standards, working principles, and research practices expected within the group.

The goal is not to create unnecessary rigidity, but to establish a shared understanding of how research should be conducted, how responsibilities should be handled, and how members are expected to contribute to a productive, respectful, and well-organized research environment.

The expectations documents complement the onboarding and data management guidelines. Together, they help define not only **what** work is done in the lab, but also **how** that work should be approached.

## What these documents cover

The expectations documents typically address topics such as:

- professionalism, communication, and reliability;
- ownership of work and research initiative;
- quality standards in experiments, code, and documentation;
- collaboration, authorship, and team conduct;
- reporting, continuity, and responsible management of research outputs.

## Core expectations

Across all roles, some core expectations remain the same:

- communicate early when blocked;
- document your work continuously;
- maintain your repositories and files cleanly;
- be reliable with deadlines and meetings;
- ask questions after making a serious first attempt.

## Professional expectation

Good research practice includes good documentation, not only good technical output.

Clear communication, organized work, and responsible collaboration are considered essential parts of research quality in the lab.
