# Lab Manual — Overview

The **Lab Manual** serves as a central reference for all members of the DISC Laboratory. It gathers the key documents, guidelines, and practices that define how the group operates on a daily basis.

The purpose of this section is to:

- provide clear and consistent guidance on how research activities are conducted within the lab;
- ensure alignment across all members in terms of expectations, workflows, and standards;
- support efficient onboarding of new members;
- promote good practices in research, collaboration, and organization.

## Contents

The Lab Manual includes documents such as:

- **Onboarding guidelines**  
  Instructions for new members to integrate into the lab and understand the main tools, platforms, and workflows.

- **Data and project management guidelines**  
  Standards for organizing files, managing data, documenting experiments, and maintaining reproducibility.

- **Expectations documents**  
  Role-specific expectations (e.g., PhD students, visiting researchers, postdoctoral researchers) outlining responsibilities, working principles, and performance standards.

## Intention

The Lab Manual is not intended to be restrictive or exhaustive. Instead, it provides a structured foundation that:

- reduces ambiguity in how work is organized and conducted;
- facilitates collaboration and continuity across projects;
- supports the production of high-quality, reproducible research.

Members are encouraged to consult the Lab Manual regularly and to contribute to its improvement when relevant.
